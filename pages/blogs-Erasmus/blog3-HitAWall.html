I've Hit the Wall

I only have a few more days left in Stockholm before I move back home for the summer. The sun is shining, the breeze is cool and I have an exam in two days which is causing me to lose what little time I have left in Stockholm looking at the inside of the university library. After having endured over five months of the cold, icy temperatures, I feel cheated that I have been given such a monumental workload just as the weather turns pleasant.

I have made the most of my time here in Stockholm. I’ve met a lot of people, experienced new things and seen new places, but now is the time to go. I want out of here. I miss the little things about England. Seeing signs in shop windows which I can understand. Milk in proper cartens. Living in a city where I don’t need to travel twenty minutes on the tube just to get to the centre. Stockholm will always stay with me but England is my home.

With the one final exam looming, I have settled into a stultifying routine which has caused me to lose all motivation and excitement for computer science, the main campus and Stockholm in general. I feel like I am simply killing time here. Revision is my main purpose at the moment, however the unproductive rut I find myself in prohibits me from doing anything worthwhile. I know my priorities but when I have only the vestiges of concentration in this regard, I find my days are withering away with little revision accomplished. I have many projects which I want to complete over the summer which I could start, but that makes me feel guilty that I have the motivation to start those but not sit down and learn all about cyber-security policy strategies. Sounds exciting, huh.

As mentioned in a previous blog, I spent the majority of April writing my book - or at least the first draft of my book. My first ever attempt at completing a project of that size. I have the structure and have written approximately 50,000 words but I want to finish this over the summer to turn it from the mere ramblings of an aspiring author into a revered novel which is on par with other professional publishings. I could have started proofreading the work which I have done this month but I want to dedicate all my time and focus on that project so I chose to wait until I have no more exams or coursework on my course.

My second project for the summer is to build a couple of websites and update my personal portfolio website. I think these will be enjoyable tasks enabling me to develop my skills and provide a valued break from editing my hundreds of pages of writings. Again, I didn’t start these projects for fear that they would distract me from revision and coding.

All of these projects are expected to take a lot of time which doesn’t concern me but with my current residence less than twenty minutes away from the Swedish capital, I want to see every nook and cranny which Stockholm has to offer. I have covered the north islands: Ostermalm, Norrmalm and Vasagatan, if you want to look them up on a map. The heart of Stockholm, Gamla Stan, is the tourist hotspot with all the tat shops lining the island and the royal palace and the official municipal buildings encompassing the thoroughfares. This was the normal route which I walked even during the peak of winter where the temperatures lingered around minus twenty degrees. The south of Stockholm encompasses the business centres and well-known technology companies such as Electronic Arts, Netflix, and AirBnB. I have walked around these places but not as much as I would have liked. With the weather only improving in the past three weeks, I haven’t had the luxury of exploring these places to the degree which I would have liked but I still feel satisfied with what I have seen.

In conclusion, with my motivation at an all time nadir, I need to get this exam out the way and leave Sweden to recuperate and refresh myself in my home country. I feel burnt out. In my final few days, I have a decision to make. Do I lock myself in the university library to ace this last exam or do I walk the city as a final valediction to my Erasmus year. I hope I have already acquired the necessary credits to pass this year so I might as well enjoy the final couple of days because the revision is proving to be onerous and at this point, I will gladly take any excursions to indulge my procrastination.
