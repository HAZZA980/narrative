Why would anyone choose to live in a cold climate?

Living in Stockholm has been an eye-opening experience for me, offering a profound understanding of the city's history and culture. However, the one aspect that I can't seem to embrace is the relentless cold weather. Stepping outside, even for a moment, feels like a painful ordeal. Initially, I had romanticized the idea of living in a snowy country, envisioning picturesque scenes of snowflakes gently falling from the sky, constant access to hot chocolate, and snowmen adorning the city streets like welcoming hosts. But these idyllic images have been overshadowed by the harsh reality of enduring sub-zero temperatures day in and day out.

When I first arrived in Stockholm, the weather was surprisingly mild. I could explore the city comfortably in a short-sleeved shirt, enjoying a local pastry and reading a few chapters of a book. This serene routine came to an abrupt end in late October when temperatures plummeted from pleasant double digits to bone-chilling negatives. Shortly thereafter, snow covered every surface, and now I can't venture outside without bundling up in multiple layers, topped with a hat, gloves, and scarf, just to stave off the constant cold.

I must say, it's quite impressive how native Swedes manage to maintain a sense of style while staying warm. In contrast, I feel like a disheveled yeti emerging from a cave whenever I enter a classroom or a coffee shop. The truth is, us Brits are not well-equipped for such frigid climates, and I find myself going through an excessive amount of tissues, which undoubtedly takes a toll on the environment. I fear that I'll be spending most of this winter cocooned in the cozy confines of my room. Fortunately, my coursework can be completed online, and I can get my daily dose of vitamin D by basking in the fleeting sunlight between 10 am and 2 pm through my window. It almost feels like I'll be hibernating, only venturing out for my weekly grocery shopping, which will undoubtedly be a challenging endeavor. By the time Christmas comes around, and I return home, I'll likely have adapted to the Swedish weather to the point where I might find myself wearing short sleeves in the chilly single-digit temperatures of the UK. People keep telling me to savor this unique experience while it lasts since snow in the UK is a rarity. Thankfully, I haven't encountered the stage where everything turns icy and slushy—yet.
