The First Week in Stockholm

Arriving in Stockholm marked the beginning of an exciting new chapter. My first impressions were a mix of excitement and minor challenges as I navigated the city’s urban landscape and settled into my new home.

Upon my arrival, I found myself exploring the local area, starting with the Kista Galleria. The mall offered a glimpse into the daily life of Stockholm, and though my quest for bedding proved fruitless at first, the adventure was far from dull. My initial accommodations were less than ideal—a makeshift pillow fashioned from hoodies and a rather uncomfortable sofa bed—but it was all part of the journey. The subsequent day was dedicated to addressing the practicalities of my new life. Although the local public transport system was ostensibly efficient, it proved to be a costly venture. A journey to IKEA, imperative for securing bedding, evolved into an odyssey of its own. The bus rides, extending up to 45 minutes with frequent delays, were a test of patience. Nevertheless, I successfully acquired the essential items. Additionally, procuring a prepaid SIM card proved to be a relatively straightforward task.

As the days unfolded, a semblance of routine began to emerge. A room inspection enabled me to unpack and organize my living space, offering a sense of normalcy. Navigating Swedish food packaging, however, posed its own challenges, underscoring the practical difficulties of living abroad. A minor administrative error with my address at the Swedish migration agency added another layer of complexity to the adjustment process, yet it was all part of the learning curve. Football quickly became a recurring theme in my week. From watching the Women’s World Cup matches in my room—where I witnessed Australia’s victory over Sweden and England’s narrow loss to Spain—to engaging with fellow students at DSV, the sport became a focal point of my social interactions. Meeting peers from diverse backgrounds, including Austria, Germany, France, Sudan, Kazakhstan, and Singapore, was a highlight of the week. Despite the diverse modules we would be taking, the connections made were a promising start to building a network.

The social aspect of my Erasmus experience continued with a Unity house meeting, where the communal rules were laid out. The event revealed the community’s spirit, though it also highlighted some of the early tensions, such as the unfortunate incident with the ping-pong table. Despite a somewhat awkward social gathering later on, the promise of future interactions at events like the Frescati sports centre offered hope for making new friends. By the end of the week, the rhythm of life in Stockholm began to settle. Cooking a burger in the communal kitchen felt like a small victory in adapting to a new environment. As I reflect on these initial days, it’s clear that while the journey has been filled with both triumphs and trials, it’s all part of the adventure of living and studying in a new city.
