Sonnet 1


For those who can see short days forthcoming
Witness the mightiness of the earnest;
Long nights are for those, the unbecoming
Hopeful reparations once be furnished.

For the tapestry of time is fickle,
Take heed to those who dwell in her presence,
The nights are lost with only a prickle
In hope, retrieving lost adolescence.

Hold thy breath in hope of the days to come
Affinities muted to those long gone.
Veracity is true for those outcomes
Hold close for brazen friends to look upon.

Time is unforgiving, in summers dawn
Death's incessant shine, controlling his pawns.

Sonnet 2


From winter to winter, death burgeons near,
Ignorant of year, progeny or blood.
Hubris and solipsis only grows fear,
Extricate or liberate if thy could.

Hades manacles to stop wanderers
fleeing and flying free in the west wind.
Mine's eye in the eye of thy ponderers,
defeated in the burden of thy sinn'd.

Black guise disguise human's prudent judgement,
Forever vindicated of man's urge.
Lonely darkness shrouds living adjustment,
Fabled Deities watch in sullen purge.

For all to witness, for the gods to bear,
Belief is naive or is it to scare?

Sonnet 3


The end has surpassed the need to go on,
The purpose is done for once and for all.
My purgatory is forever gone
and yet thy will too is fabled to fall

The ice and the snow falls once more;
an idyllic beauty I've failed to see.
Heaven for the rich and not for the poor,
Oh, cold gasps, the place I desire to be.

A place so sweet I long to see again,
methinks a change of scene is earnt once more.
Memories fade, replaced with fog and pain,
A new Tabula rasa to explore.

Now out with the old and onto the new
Youth is precious and so I think are you

Sonnet 4


For me, mine's own future shines so brightly,
Though the emnity of one's mighty dreams.
Those small impacts and big hopes change slightly,
Introspective voices morph into screams.

Harried and hastened for mine's decision;
Hoping for the sweet relief of long rest.
Afterall, it takes a man of vision,
to see and achieve all which is the best.

Have moment to witness to be present
Temerity is boldness to be well.
Journeys are challenges to be content,
Retrospection says it must be farewell.

Fear is the thorn which we all must conquer
free ourselves from our eternal anchor

Sonnet 5


With heavy bones and eyes so deep, thou weep
A wanderer in the perpetual dark
From silent screams to tears that softly seep
In pain, transforming flint into a spark

Ah, lonelier then and lonelier now
But how long must thy reverie await?
Destiny's cruel but honors thy vow
Hitherto, one's affliction is too great

Fight back, honorable tribulation
Fear me for years and years in search of life
The temerity of thy creation;
The tears drop only from her bloody knife.

Suffering and pain contrived from one's own
Hear me at last, succumbed to my groan.

Sonnet 6


It's time to take respite from this long road,
A journey so tiresome it will be long believed.
Days have passed but the memories are sowed,
Take heed from penance which is long received.

From whence once came the road has been so far,
Destined for greatness though no hope accrued.
Ah, what had shone so bright from sun to star,
The future is fleeting to be renewed.

For now, belief is for the credulous,
Written by the fatalists in quiet dawn.
These oaths spurned, known to the Perfidious,
Beauteous mornings for which God had drawn.

No hope, no belief for solitude's ear,
Wander'st in the shade, sheds final tear.

Sonnet 7


What is love but just a fickle mistress,
There to torment and torture weak beings?
It spreads and inculcates like a sickness,
Ready to control and usurp feelings.

Mercurial girl, why go through the pain
But for your affection and quaint smile?
Please do exhibit what there is to gain
Only your lies and guise just to beguile.

Caprice and hubris, lady of the night,
Look and see, for your gait is worth the wait.
Beware, foul demon, for lover’s deep bite;
The dawn is nigh, and my chance doth too late.

Obey these futures, allow man to dream,
Thou lonely life is not all what it seems.

Sonnet 8


Tired is mine’s eye which flickers in the night
Dreams a dream of hopeful exuberance
Second to second to mornin’s first light
Dawn will break with faiths honest assurance

Dawn to dusk like nature’s changin’ season
Steal from the gods honour in rhyming tone
Much to barter and yet all to reason
Dancing reveries much enjoyed alone

But each one must wake for life to move on
To bear witness to ever moving sun
Hopeless mind come back but for all is gone?
Awaken angel for the night is done.

Bright is the light of the days yet to pass.
Belief is the angel within dark mass.

Sonnet 9


Long gone are the days of youthful despair,
with fear and fright shining in children's eye
Now here come the days of maternal care.
With love and hope hiding a brutal lie.

Consecrated life so woven in blood,
the mistruthen aspects of one's belief;
The devils mendacity when in love,
Confess your sins for cathartic relief.

Clear is the mind ready for the next day
hither and thither is the new world breadth
follow the heart, follow will to obey
to protect against one's own shatter'd cleft

Trust in the body as one does in mind
Trust in oneself for loathsome love is kind
